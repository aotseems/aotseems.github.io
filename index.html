📗 Sorting Guide
Everything you need to know about each bin.
💡
The golden rule
When in doubt, throw it out — into the general waste bin. Contaminated recycling ruins an entire batch.
♻️
Recycling Bin
Clean, single-material items only
For items made of one material. The key word is clean — food residue contaminates entire batches.
✅ What goes in
🥤Plastic bottles
📰Newspapers
🥫Metal cans
📦Cardboard
❌ Do NOT put in
🍕Greasy pizza boxes
🧻Soiled tissues
🍂
Compost Bin
Organic waste only
For food scraps and plant-based material. Avoid meat and dairy — they attract pests.
✅ What goes in
🍌Fruit & veg
☕Coffee grounds
🥚Eggshells
🌿Garden clippings
❌ Do NOT put in
🍗Meat, fish, dairy
🧈Oily food waste
🗑️
Landfill Bin
Contaminated single-material items
For waste made of one material but contaminated or unsuitable for recycling.
✅ What goes in
🧻Used tissues
🩹Used plasters
🖊️Broken pens
🚬Cigarette butts
❌ Do NOT put in
📱Electronics — use E-Waste
🛍️
General Waste
Multi-material items
For composite items made of two or more bonded materials that cannot be separated.
✅ What goes in
🛍️Chip packets
📦Bubble wrap
🧴Toothpaste tubes
🫧Styrofoam
❌ Do NOT put in
🥤Clean plastics — use Recycle
📱
E-Waste Drop-Off
Electronics — never in regular bins
Electronic waste contains toxic materials. E-waste must go to a certified drop-off.
📱Smartphones
💻Laptops
🔋All batteries
🔌Chargers & cables
⚠️ Illegal in many countries to put e-waste in regular bins.
